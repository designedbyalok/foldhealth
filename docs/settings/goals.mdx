The **Goals** settings page (`#/settings/agents/goals`) is where you define what each care program is trying to achieve. Goals drive completion tracking on the patient worklist and feed the Care Coordination analytics view.

## When to use it

- Stand up a new care program (TCM, chronic disease, preventive screening).
- Adjust the weighted score that determines when a patient is considered "complete".
- Audit who changed a goal and when.

## Goal categories

| Category    | What it measures                                                       |
|-------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| TCM         | Transitional Care Management touchpoints after a discharge.            |
| Outreach    | Patient outreach attempts and completion rate.                          |
| Onboarding  | Steps to enroll a patient into a program.                               |
| Preventive  | Wellness visits, screenings, and HEDIS-aligned tasks.                   |
| Billing     | Billable touchpoints required to close a care episode.                  |

## Create a goal

The **Goal Wizard** is a four-step drawer:

1. **Identity** — name, category, and description.
2. **Steps** — the ordered tasks that make up the goal.
3. **Scoring** — weight each step so partial completion still scores.
4. **Review** — confirm and save.

After saving, the goal becomes available to assign to patients in the relevant programs.

## Edit a goal

Select a row in the Goals table to open the **Goal Detail** drawer. Adjust steps, weights, or status. Every change is written to the audit log with the editor's name and timestamp.

## Audit log

Open the history icon on any row to see the timeline of changes, including status flips (e.g. **Enabled → Disabled** shown as a colored badge transition) and text edits (shown as strikethrough old → new value).
